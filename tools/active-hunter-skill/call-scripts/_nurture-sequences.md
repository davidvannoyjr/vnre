# No-Answer Nurture Sequences

For targets who don't connect on the call. The Active-Hunter skill drafts these as
Gmail/text drafts (approval-gated) and logs the attempt to FUB. Goal: stay in front of them
until they pick up or opt out — no lead leaks from a missed dial.

> Compliance: honor the `Opt-Out` tag everywhere; texts need prior consent; mind KS/MO
> calling/texting hours. Stop the sequence on any reply or opt-out.

## FSBO / Expired (high intent — 5 touches over ~2 weeks)
1. **Day 0 (after no-answer), text:** "{name}, David Van Noy / Van Noy RE — tried you about
   {street}. I put together what it'd net you sold vs. FSBO. Want me to send it?"
2. **Day 2, email:** the net-sheet comparison + 2 recent {city} sales. CTA: 20-min appointment.
3. **Day 5, call** (retry, different time of day).
4. **Day 9, text:** "Still planning to move? If timing changed, no worries — just tell me when."
5. **Day 14, email:** market update for their street + soft "when you're ready" close → then
   drop to monthly.

## Aged Lead (medium — 3 touches over ~10 days)
1. **Day 0, text:** "Still in the market, or did plans change? One word back tells me how to help."
2. **Day 4, email:** a fresh curated list (buyer) or a value estimate (owner).
3. **Day 10:** if no reply, tag the presumed timeline and move to monthly market emails.

## COI / Past Client (relationship — no pressure)
- Don't "sequence" them like leads. One friendly follow-up text, then hand off to the
  **retention engine**, which carries equity / anniversary / referral touches on cadence.

## Templates live in DVN voice
Direct, declarative, no "just checking in" filler, no "hope this finds you well." Every message
ends with one specific next step.
