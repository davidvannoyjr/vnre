# Board of Advisors — Interview Guide

Claude runs this once with DVN to stand up the board, then re-runs the short
version whenever the board's purpose shifts. Direct, blunt, no warm-up — DVN's
register.

## Part 1 — What the board is for (10 minutes)
1. Name the three decisions you make most often that you'd want a second brain on.
2. For each: what does a *good* version of that decision look like 12 months later?
3. Where do you currently get counsel, and where does it fall short?
4. What kind of advice do you *not* want? (e.g. motivational, theoretical,
   anything not ROI-positive in 90 days — the master-manual decision filter
   applies to the board too.)

## Part 2 — Who's on it (15 minutes)
5. Whose thinking do you already absorb — podcasts, books, YouTube, people you've
   worked with?
6. For each name: is there *accessible, real* material? (book, long-form talk,
   written framework). **No accessible source → not on the board.** A vibe is not
   a profile.
7. Who covers a lens you're weak on, not just one you already agree with? Build the
   board to argue, not to nod.
8. Cap at 3–7. More than 7 and the synthesis turns to mush.

## Part 3 — Per advisor, capture the profile (build `advisors.json`)
For each pick, fill the registry fields from the source material:
- **decision_lens** — the first question they always ask.
- **heuristics** — their rules of thumb, in their words.
- **signature_moves** — what they reliably do.
- **will_flag** — the risk they always catch.
- **voice** — how they talk (drives fidelity).
- **sources** — the real material the profile is built from. Required.

## The decision template (drop into `decision.md` for each consult)

# Decision: <one line>

## Context
<one paragraph — what's going on, what's at stake, the numbers if any>
