The difference between an agent who gets garbage from ChatGPT and one who gets gold is setup. Do this once.

## 1. Get the right account

Use a paid plan. The free model is weaker and you'll judge AI by its worst version. $20/mo against one extra listing is not a decision.

## 2. Tell it who you are — permanently

Open **Settings → Personalization → Custom Instructions**. Paste a version of this:

> I'm a residential real estate listing agent. Write in a direct, confident, professional voice — short sentences, no hype, no clichés, no "I'd be happy to." Assume I'm the expert. When I give you property or client details, use only those facts. Never invent features, prices, or comps.

Now every response starts in your voice instead of sounding like a press release.

## 3. Learn the one habit that matters: context first

Bad prompt: *"Write a listing description."*

Good prompt: *"Write a listing description for a 4-bed/3-bath ranch in Leawood, KS. Updated kitchen, finished basement, half-acre lot, top school district. Buyer is a move-up family. 150 words, my voice."*

The rule: **AI is only as good as the facts you hand it.** Vague in, vague out.

## Your win this week

Set custom instructions, then draft three things you'd normally write by hand — a listing description, a just-sold post, and a follow-up email — feeding real details each time. Time yourself. That number is your new baseline.
