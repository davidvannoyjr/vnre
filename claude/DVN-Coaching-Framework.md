# The DVN Coaching Framework — The 22-Point System

> **This is THE DVN Coaching framework.** It replaces the earlier five-stage
> model (Forge / Conquer / Anchor / Architect / Own). Everything DVN Coaching
> teaches, scores, and holds a client accountable to lives inside these 22 points.

The business of selling real estate is not 1,000 things. It's 22. Master the 22
and the production follows. Ignore any one of them and that's where the income
leaks out. This framework is the whole job on one page — the disciplines that
build a 75-listing-a-year operation, written as standards, not theory.

It is **dual-purpose by design:**

- **For DVN** — the operating standard for Van Noy Real Estate. The bar I hold
  *myself* to, daily.
- **For coaching clients** — the curriculum and the scoreboard. Every producing
  agent gets scored 1–10 on all 22 points. The lowest scores are the coaching
  plan. We attack weakness in order, build the discipline, then move to the next.

---

## How to use it

**As a discipline (self):** these are non-negotiable standards, not suggestions.
The schedule, the call count, the ratios — they're either happening or they're
not. No motivation required. The system carries you.

**As a diagnostic (clients):** score the agent 1–10 on each point. A 9–10 is a
built, automatic discipline. A 1–4 is a hole costing them deals right now. You
don't coach all 22 at once — you find the two or three lowest, fix those to a 7+,
then re-score. Repetition builds mastery. One point at a time, to the standard.

**The decision filter** — every point in this system passes all four. So does
anything you add to a client's plan:

1. Does it drive listings, GCI, or coaching revenue?
2. Is it ROI-positive within 90 days?
3. Is it simple enough to execute daily?
4. Does it remove friction or add it?

If a tactic fails the filter, it's not in the framework. Theoretical, complex, or
motivation-dependent ideas don't make the cut.

---

## The six pillars

The 22 points run in a deliberate arc — who you are, how you generate, how you
convert, how you serve and systematize, how you build the business, and the edge
that compounds all of it.

| Pillar | Points | What it builds |
|---|---|---|
| **I. Identity & Foundation** | 1–3 | The operator and the calendar that make everything else possible |
| **II. Lead Generation** | 4–6 | The engine — where the business comes from, every day |
| **III. Conversion** | 7–14 | Turning conversations into signed listings and controlled sales |
| **IV. Service & Systems** | 15–18 | Keeping clients for life and making the work repeatable |
| **V. Business & Growth** | 19–21 | Leverage, money, and the plan that scales it |
| **VI. The Edge** | 22 | AI infrastructure — the multiplier on the other 21 |

# Pillar I — Identity & Foundation

## 1. Mindset & Identity
**Principle.** Who you are drives what you do. You don't rise to your goals; you
fall to your identity. A salesperson who *is* a prospector prospects — no
willpower spent. Production is the output of identity, skills, activities, and
action, held together by discipline and consistency.

**The standard.** Treat this as a $200,000+/year profession and show up like it.
Controlled emotions are an asset; uncontrolled emotions are a liability. The only
"no" that counts is the last one before you quit.

**Daily execution.** Decide who you are before the day starts. Protect the
self-talk. Energy, enthusiasm, and intensity at a 9 out of 10 — every
appointment, every call block.

**Coaching application.** Most stalls labeled "skill" or "market" problems are
identity problems. Find what the agent believes about themselves and the work.
Rebuild the identity first; the activities stop being a fight.

## 2. Goals & Vision
**Principle.** You can't hit a target you haven't named. Goals across all five
life areas — physical, mental, spiritual, family, money — so the business serves
the life, not the reverse.

**The standard.** Grow at least 20% per year — that doubles income every three to
four years. Written goals, working backward to a daily activity number. The plan
is in the coach's hands by November 15th for the year ahead.

**Daily execution.** Every day's call count, contact count, and appointment goal
ladders up to the annual number. The daily number *is* the goal made executable.

**Coaching application.** No vague goals. Convert "I want a better year" into
listings → presentations → appointments → contacts → daily calls. Set at least
three accountability partners. Numbers turned in weekly.

## 3. The Schedule Is the System
**Principle.** Time management is the whole game. The schedule is not a
suggestion that bends around your mood — it's the system that runs the business
when motivation isn't there.

**The standard.** Three parts to the day: morning routine and prospecting block,
early-afternoon lead/admin work, and afternoon presentation time. Five days a
week is normal; seven is a sign the systems are broken. Income-producing activity
gets 75–80% of the day.

**Daily execution.** Time-block the day in advance. Prospecting first, before the
day can hijack it. Track time hour-by-hour for two weeks to find where it leaks.

**Coaching application.** Make the agent send their calendar, not describe it.
Build one schedule SOP — the prospecting block — and enforce it before adding
anything else. Protect the block; everything else is negotiable.

# Pillar II — Lead Generation

## 4. The Prospecting Engine
**Principle.** 80% of your business should come from going and *finding* it, not
waiting for it. Prospecting is the single highest-paid activity in the business.
It is not optional and it is not seasonal.

**The standard.** A minimum of **3 hours of prospecting daily** — roughly **150
dials, ~30 contacts** — no matter what the goal is. A minimum of **10–12 contacts
per hour.** Expireds and FSBOs worked at the high-pickup windows (around 8am,
noon, 3pm, 7pm). At ~50 contacts you earn 1 appointment; ~70% of appointments
become listings. That ratio is the entire forecast.

**Daily execution.** Same block, every day, before anything else. Scripts in
front of you. Count contacts, not just dials. Hit the number, then stop and go
list.

**Coaching application.** This is the point most agents score lowest and avoid
hardest. Hold the contact count daily — it's the leading indicator of every dollar
downstream. No appointment shortage survives a real prospecting block.

## 5. The Database Is the Business
**Principle.** Your past clients and centers of influence are the highest-ROI
asset you own. The under-worked database is almost always the #1 opportunity in
the business.

**The standard.** Build to **3,000+ contacts, tiered A/B/C.** **10% will give you
business every year if you ask** — 3,000 contacts is ~300 transactions of
latent demand. Speak to every past client and COI **four times a year** by phone,
plus mail/touch four times a year. Communication runs ~60% conversation, 40%
information. Anyone you pay money to on a regular basis belongs in the database.

**Daily execution.** Add 5+ qualified names a day. Work the A-tier on a tight
cycle (every 4–6 weeks). Post-close call rhythm: day of closing, 7 days, 14 days,
then onto the long-term cycle.

**Coaching application.** Most agents have a database and don't work it. Get the
real count and the tiering first. Re-engaging the under-contacted tier is usually
the fastest 90-day GCI win on the board.

## 6. The Follow-Up Discipline
**Principle.** The fortune is in the follow-up, but only if you define what a lead
actually is. A lead is **a person who will sign a contract within seven days** —
everything else is database.

**The standard.** Convert a lead within 7 days or move it to the database. The
**3/3/3 rule:** 3 rings then hang up; if no contact after 3 attempts, release it;
if 3 conversations produce no appointment, release it. 100 great leads a year is
all it takes to close 100 transactions. Speed-to-lead on every inbound — minutes,
not hours.

**Daily execution.** Review the active lead stack every Monday. Work it daily.
Don't let "maybe someday" prospects clog the lead system — demote them and keep
the lead list clean and hot.

**Coaching application.** Agents confuse leads with contacts and chase cold names
for months. Enforce the 7-day definition and the 3/3/3 release. A clean lead
system is what makes follow-up feel light instead of overwhelming.

# Pillar III — Conversion

## 7. 100% Pre-Qualification
**Principle.** Pre-qualifying is the most leveraged 10 minutes in the business.
It protects your time and pre-loads the close. You earn the right to present by
qualifying first — every time.

**The standard.** Pre-qualify **100% of prospects, 100% of the time, with 100% of
the questions — no exceptions.** Every buyer is qualified by a lender before you
get in the car. The goal: list **75% of the people you present to** because you
only present to the qualified and motivated.

**Daily execution.** Run the full pre-qual on every appointment before it's set.
Motivation, timeline, price expectation, decision-makers — surfaced before you
invest a minute of presentation time.

**Coaching application.** Skipping pre-qual is why presentations fail and
appointments get cancelled. Make it 100% or nothing — a partial pre-qual is the
same as none. This single discipline lifts close rate more than any script.

## 8. The Listing Presentation
**Principle.** The listing appointment is where the business is won. Show up
prepared, lead the conversation, and tailor everything to their motivation. They
hire the authority in the room.

**The standard.** The presentation stays **under 30 minutes** — past 30 you've
shifted from selling to telling. First impression is made in the first 15–30
seconds. Answer their three real questions: how much, how long, and **what will
you actually DO** — with 5–6 specific, concrete actions. The full presentation
process: pre-qualify 100%, deliver the pre-listing package, confirm the
appointment, show up prepared physically/mentally/emotionally, and follow the
dialogue.

**Daily execution.** Pre-listing package out ahead of every appointment.
Presentation tight, visual, and motivation-led. Ask for the business at the
table.

**Coaching application.** Most agents over-talk and under-prepare. Tighten the
presentation, cut it under 30 minutes, and drill the "what I do" list until it's
specific and automatic. Role-play it before they walk into the real one.

## 9. Pricing to Sell
**Principle.** A listing priced wrong is a slow loss disguised as a win. Your job
is to price it to move and to lock in the correction before you ever need it.

**The standard.** **Get the price reduction pre-signed at the time of listing.**
The market reads the price: lots of showings and no offers means overpriced; no
showings at all means badly overpriced. Lead with data, not opinion.

**Daily execution.** Bring the CMA and the net sheet to the table. Set price and
the reduction schedule on day one. Watch showing-to-offer signals and act on them
on the agreed schedule — no emotional debates later.

**Coaching application.** Agents buy listings with high prices and then carry dead
inventory. Teach the pre-signed reduction and the showing/offer read. Priced-right
inventory is what makes the rest of the system pay off.

## 10. Communication & Personality Styles
**Principle.** People buy from people who communicate in *their* style, not yours.
Reading the style and adapting to it — versatility — is the skill that closes the
gap between a good agent and a trusted one.

**The standard.** Four styles: **Analytical** (facts, data, precision — give them
options and proof), **Driver** (fast, bottom-line — get to the point, ask don't
demand), **Amiable** (relationship, reassurance — slow down, who else is
involved), **Expressive** (vision, energy — paint the future, keep it moving).
Six ways to build versatility: ask questions, listen, quit needing to be right,
remove ego, get out of your box, put yourself in uncomfortable situations.

**Daily execution.** Tag every contact's style in the CRM. Open each
conversation in their style, not your default. Adapt pace, detail, and proof to
the person across from you.

**Coaching application.** Agents present in their own style and wonder why half
their prospects don't connect. Teach the four styles, then drill versatility —
it's the multiplier on presentation, objection handling, and negotiation alike.

## 11. Objection Mastery
**Principle.** An objection is a question in the customer's mind that hasn't been
answered yet — not a rejection. Handled calmly, it's a buying signal.

**The standard.** You need only **two or three strong responses to each common
objection** — known cold, not improvised. Always agree, smile, nod, and never
argue. The objection is information; the argument is a lost deal.

**Daily execution.** Know the common objections and your responses by heart.
Stay relaxed when they come. Answer the real question underneath, then move to the
close.

**Coaching application.** Agents freeze or argue because they're improvising.
Drill the handful of responses until they're automatic. Confidence on objections
comes from repetition, not personality.

## 12. Closing — Asking for the Business
**Principle.** You have to ask. Most sales happen well past the first ask, and the
agent who's comfortable asking — directly and more than once — wins the listing.

**The standard.** Be ready to **close a minimum of five times** — roughly 80% of
sales happen after the fifth close. Ask plainly: "Will you sign the contract,
please?" The only "no" that counts is the last one before you stop asking.

**Daily execution.** Ask for the business at every appointment. Treat early "no"s
as not-yet, not never. Stay comfortable in the silence after the ask.

**Coaching application.** Most agents ask once, get an objection, and retreat.
Teach the multiple-close as normal and expected. The ask is a learnable
discipline, not a personality trait — drill it.

## 13. Negotiation
**Principle.** The transaction is not about you. The agent who keeps emotion out
and serves the client's outcome negotiates from strength.

**The standard.** Controlled emotions are an asset; uncontrolled emotions are a
liability. Don't let your commission get dragged into the negotiation. Represent
the client's interest, not your ego.

**Daily execution.** Prepare the position before the conversation. Stay calm,
factual, and outcome-focused. Defend value with data, not pressure.

**Coaching application.** Agents personalize negotiations and lose leverage.
Teach emotional control and commission discipline. Calm and prepared beats
aggressive and reactive every time.

## 14. Buyer Control
**Principle.** Buyers are run on *your* system or they run *you*. The leverage is
in working the right buyers, on your terms, with the listing business as the
priority.

**The standard.** Target the **AAA buyers:** motivated past clients who have to
buy, sellers already priced to sell, and highly qualified, highly motivated
referrals. **Show three homes at a time.** Run the business at roughly **80%
listings / 20% controlled buyer sales.** Every buyer pre-qualified by a lender
and met at the office, not the house.

**Daily execution.** Qualify buyer motivation hard. Meet at the office, set the
terms, show three. Don't burn prospecting time chauffeuring unqualified buyers.

**Coaching application.** Agents drown in unqualified buyer work and starve their
listing business. Install buyer control: AAA criteria, office meeting, three at a
time. It frees the time the listing engine needs.

# Pillar IV — Service & Systems

## 15. Customers for Life
**Principle.** The sale is the start of the relationship, not the end. Service
turns one transaction into a referral annuity for the life of the client.

**The standard.** Stay in contact on a real rhythm — calling every ~10 days at the
peak of a transaction; a minimum of four meaningful touches a year afterward.
Under-promise, over-deliver. Reliability, dependability, honesty — every time, no
exceptions.

**Daily execution.** Run the post-close service cadence by system, not memory.
Deliver more than you promised on the small things. Ask for the referral once
you've earned it.

**Coaching application.** Agents chase new leads while ignoring the goldmine of
past clients. Build the service cadence into the CRM so it runs automatically.
Retention and referral are the cheapest growth there is.

## 16. Scripts & Skill-Building
**Principle.** Professionals practice. The script isn't a crutch — it's the
freedom to stop thinking about *what* to say and focus on the person. Skill is
built by repetition, not by reading.

**The standard.** Practice and role-play before live calls and appointments —
daily. Handwrite the listing presentation dialogue regularly until it's owned, not
memorized. Work on the weakest scripts first.

**Daily execution.** Role-play the day's scripts before the day's calls. Drill the
two weakest objections and the presentation open every morning.

**Coaching application.** The agents who refuse to practice are the ones who plateau.
Make role-play a scheduled, non-negotiable part of the week. Skill is the only
thing that compounds faster than the database.

## 17. Tracking the Numbers
**Principle.** What gets measured gets managed. You can't fix a funnel you can't
see. The scoreboard is how discipline becomes visible — and coachable.

**The standard.** Track, at minimum: **total contacts, hours prospected,
presentations, listings taken, listings sold, buyer sales, closings.** Turn the
numbers in weekly. The ratios (50 contacts → 1 appointment, ~70% appointment →
listing) come straight off this board and drive every forecast.

**Daily execution.** Log the numbers daily — contacts and hours at minimum. Review
the ratios weekly to see where the funnel leaks.

**Coaching application.** No numbers, no coaching. The weekly scoreboard is the
accountability spine of every engagement. When an agent's results dip, the board
shows exactly which point in this framework broke.

## 18. Systematize Everything
**Principle.** If it only works when you do it personally, it's not a business —
it's a job with stress. Systems make the work repeatable, delegable, and durable.

**The standard.** Build a defined system for each core area: past clients/COI,
lead follow-up, listing property, transaction follow-up, price reductions, the
hiring process, and keeping track of all your materials. Each system has an owner,
a trigger, and a clear "done."

**Daily execution.** Run the SOPs, don't improvise. When something breaks twice,
write the system so it can't break a third time.

**Coaching application.** Find the three SOPs that unlock the most of the agent's
own time and build those first. Systems are what let a producer become an owner —
and what make this whole framework survive a busy week.

# Pillar V — Business & Growth

## 19. Delegation & Leverage
**Principle.** Your time is the constraint. The leverage is in handing off
everything that isn't dollar-productive so you can stay on the listing appointment
and the prospecting block.

**The standard.** **Hire the first assistant at ~40 transactions a year; add one
for every 40–45 after.** Pay **fair market value plus 5%** to keep quality. Keep
yourself on income-producing activity **75–80% of the day** and admin under 20%.
The assistant keeps you on schedule, handles the paperwork, and handles incoming
calls so you stay in front of sellers.

**Daily execution.** Each role owns a lane — not just touches it. Daily rhythm:
morning standup, midday check, end-of-day report. Push every non-dollar task off
your plate.

**Coaching application.** Agents either hire too early (no production to support
it) or too late (drowning, capped). Use the 40-transaction trigger as the
objective line. Then define the role's ownership in writing before the first day.

## 20. Money Management & Profit
**Principle.** It's not what you make, it's what you keep. Top-line production
without profit discipline is just expensive activity. Run the personal and
business finances like a business.

**The standard.** Split the gross into accounts: **~30% business, ~30% personal,
~25% tax, ~15% savings.** Hold company expenses to **18–20%** on a traditional
split (30–35% max on a high-commission model). Keep a **six-month reserve.** When
margins tighten: cut expenses 20% or lift income 20%.

**Daily execution.** Run the account split on every closing. Know gross profit per
listing and per coaching client. Review one financial dashboard every week.

**Coaching application.** High earners with no money system are one slow quarter
from trouble. Install the account split and the reserve first. Profit per unit —
not gross production — is the real scoreboard for the business owner.

## 21. The Business Plan
**Principle.** A business without a plan is a hobby with overhead. The plan turns
the vision into a number and the number into a daily activity you can actually
execute.

**The standard.** Plan for **at least 20% growth a year.** Work backward from the
income goal to listings, to presentations, to appointments, to the daily contact
count. Map growth across its real levers: market, database, activity, marketing,
referral, team, skills, and mindset. Plan in the coach's hands by mid-November for
the coming year.

**Daily execution.** The plan lives as the daily number, reviewed weekly against
the scoreboard. Adjust activity, not ambition, when the numbers drift.

**Coaching application.** Build the plan *with* the agent so they own it, then hold
them to the daily activity it implies. The plan is only real if it survives
contact with a Tuesday — keep it tied to the numbers in Point 17.

# Pillar VI — The Edge

## 22. AI Infrastructure
**Principle.** AI is the multiplier on the other 21 points — not a replacement for
the disciplines, an amplifier of them. The agent who builds real AI infrastructure
prospects more, follows up faster, presents sharper, and serves deeper with the
same hours in the day. This is the edge that compounds, and it's what separates
the DVN Coaching framework from every system built before it.

**The standard.** Build the stack on a real CRM spine (**Follow Up Boss**) and
deploy AI against each point that has leverage — measured by the same decision
filter as everything else: does it drive GCI, pay off in 90 days, run simply, and
remove friction. No pilots that never ship. Every tool has an owner and a
30-day first project, and a clear line on what data never goes into a public model.

**The DVN stack (current).**
- **Prospecting (Pt 4):** Mojo dialer + Upfirst voice agents (Alex inbound, Jordan
  routing); AI lead scoring across Follow Up Boss / Mojo / Zillow; AI-generated
  talking points (market stats, neighborhood) before each call.
- **Database & follow-up (Pts 5–6):** smart cadences segmented by motivation and
  personality style; auto-summary of a contact's last touches before the call;
  re-engagement triggers aimed at the under-contacted 3,000; lead-source ROI
  dashboard.
- **Presentation & pricing (Pts 8–9):** AI-generated CMA in ~60 seconds; real-time
  absorption, price-per-sqft, and days-on-market visuals; personalized marketing
  plan generated ahead of the appointment; voice-memo debrief to AI right after.
- **Skill-building (Pt 16):** AI role-play partner drilling the weakest scripts and
  objections every morning; a custom GPT trained on the DVN scripts, voice, and
  the "100 Ways to Add 25 Deals" IP.
- **Marketing (Pt 15):** AI listing descriptions, image enhancement, video tours,
  Just-Listed/Just-Sold short-form at scale — automate the production, never
  automate the relationship.
- **Coaching (DVN Coaching):** AI accountability tools tracking client call counts
  and schedule adherence; course/book/IP creation; lead-magnet engine for the
  coaching brand.

**Daily execution.** AI handles the repetitive and the analytical; the human keeps
the relationship and the close. Review the AI tool inventory regularly — what's
working, what's a toy, what's a distraction — and kill the worst, double the best.

**Coaching application.** Don't sell agents "use AI." Bolt specific AI leverage
onto the specific point they're weakest in. Decide what only a human can do versus
what AI can augment, role out one tool at a time to the standard, and protect the
relationship the tools are there to serve.
