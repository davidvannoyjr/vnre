# White-Label / Back-End Broker Model — Legal Research & Build Plan

**For:** DVN, Broker/Owner, Van Noy Real Estate (VNRE) — Kansas City metro (Jackson County MO, Johnson County KS)
**Question:** Run VNRE as the broker of record while teams and agents underneath operate fully branded as their own companies — the "Side" model. What do the laws require? How much of the brokerage *has* to be promoted? Can the brokerage be invisible entirely?
**Date:** 2026-06
**Status:** Research complete. Decision pending.

---

## Bottom line up front

**You can build this. You cannot make the brokerage disappear — but in Missouri you can make it nearly invisible, more so than in almost any other state.**

Three hard truths from the law:

1. **A truly broker-anonymous ad is illegal in both Missouri and Kansas.** Every advertisement by a licensee must carry the licensed broker's name. Whenever an agent's name or phone appears, the broker's name *and phone* must appear too. No exceptions for teams, DBAs, or "their own company."

2. **Missouri appears to have no prominence or font-size rule** — and that gap is the whole strategy, so it must be confirmed. The actual rule text (20 CSR 2250-8.070) requires the broker name to be *present* but does not require it to be equal in size or "equally prominent." If that holds, the agent's brand leads and "Brokered by VNRE" sits in compliant fine print — exactly how Side and eXp operate. **Caveat (verify first):** a secondary compliance source surfaced a "same sized font as the Brokerage" claim for Missouri — but that language traces to **Mississippi** (also abbreviated "MREC," Admin. Rule 3.3), not Missouri, and is almost certainly cross-state contamination. No agent found a font/prominence ratio in the actual Missouri rule. This is the single highest-stakes item to confirm: if Missouri did adopt a same-size rule, the footer strategy dies and Missouri behaves like Kansas (see §3).

3. **Kansas is stricter and caps you.** In Johnson County, the agent/team name may be **no more than 2× the size** of the VNRE name and must sit **adjacent** to it online. The brokerage can be the smaller name — but not by much, and never hidden.

Every white-label operator in the country — Side, eXp, Real's Private Label, Pinnacle — lives inside these exact rules. None of them are invisible. They are *minimized and back-stage*. "White label" in real estate means the consumer-facing brand is the agent's; the legal broker of record is still named in the fine print, on the license, and in the MLS. That responsibility cannot be white-labeled away — and the broker carries the liability for everything the branded team does.

**The model is real, it's legal, and the closest direct competitors (Side, Pinnacle) do not appear to operate in Kansas or Missouri yet. That's whitespace.**

## 2. Missouri law — what must be promoted

Controlling authority: **RSMo Chapter 339** (license law) and **20 CSR 2250-8.070** ("Advertising"), plus companion rules **8.080** (franchises/trade names), **4.030** (fictitious names). Statutory teeth: **RSMo § 339.100.2(24)** makes false/misleading ads and improper team names a disciplinable offense.

> **Verification note:** Primary-source fetch was blocked this session (every .gov + legal mirror returned 403). Quotes below were extracted from the Cornell LII and Justia reproductions of the official rule, cross-checked across many independent searches — high confidence on the *substance* and rule numbers, but confirm exact subsection lettering against the SOS PDF (`sos.mo.gov/cmsimages/adrules/csr/current/20csr/20c2250-8.pdf`) before relying on it in a filing.

**The four rules that bind the model:**

1. **Broker name on every ad.** *"Every advertisement of real estate by a licensee shall contain the broker's regular business name or the name under which the broker or the broker's firm is licensed and shall indicate that the party advertising is a real estate broker and not a private party."* — 20 CSR 2250-8.070

2. **Agent contact info triggers broker contact info.** *"If the licensee's name or telephone number, or both, is used in any advertisement, the advertisement also shall include the name and telephone number of the broker or firm who holds the licensee's license."* — This is the binding floor. An agent-branded ad that shows the agent's number **must** also show VNRE's name and number.

3. **No blind ads.** A licensee may not advertise to look like a private party, may not run "for sale by owner" when a licensee is involved, and *"No real estate advertisement by a licensee shall show only a post office box number, telephone number or street address."*

4. **Team names can't pose as a brokerage.** *"No licensee or group of licensees shall advertise as a real estate company in any manner, or use any name, team name, or other term that could be construed by members of the public as the advertiser being a real estate partnership, company, brokerage, or business entity, unless the advertiser holds a valid appropriate entity license."* Words like *realty, brokerage, company* are the trigger. (Effective Oct 30, 2022.)

**The safe harbor that makes the model work:** the entity-word ban does **not** apply when the team name *incorporates the broker's name*, AND a team can use a fictitious name if it is **registered with the Missouri Secretary of State and owned by VNRE.** Critically: *"the registration of a fictitious name … does not exempt a licensee or group of licensees from the requirements of [the broker-name rule]."* Registering the DBA does not let the team drop the VNRE name.

**The Missouri advantage — there is NO prominence/font rule.** The only "prominent fashion" language in 8.070 attaches to the *owner-broker* disclosure (when a licensee sells their own property), not to the broker-name requirement. Missouri requires the VNRE name to be *present*, *readable*, and not a blind ad — but does **not** require it to be equal to, or any fixed ratio of, the team's brand. Missouri is *more permissive* than Ohio ("equal prominence"), California ("as prominently and conspicuously as the team name"), Minnesota ("more prominently than the team name"), or Kansas (2× cap). **In Missouri, "Brokered by Van Noy Real Estate" in a footer is compliant.**

## 3. Kansas overlay — the binding constraint in Johnson County

Kansas is the tighter of your two states. Authority: **K.S.A. 58-3086** (statute) + **K.A.R. 86-3-7** (regulation, effective 7/1/2020).

- **Broker name, prominent and conspicuous.** *"All advertising conducted by a licensee shall … include the name of the supervising broker's trade name or business name by prominently and conspicuously displaying or announcing [it] in a readable and identifiable manner."* — K.S.A. 58-3086(b)

- **The 2× cap + adjacency rule** (this is the one that constrains you). A salesperson's or team's name may **not**: *"(1) Use the term 'realty,' 'brokerage,' 'company,' or any other term that can be construed as a real estate company separate from the supervising broker's company; (2) [be] more than two times larger in font size than … the supervising broker's trade name or business name; or (3) [be] not adjacent to the supervising broker's trade name or business name in any internet, web site, social media, or social networking advertisement."* — K.A.R. 86-3-7(b)

- **Definition is broad.** Kansas explicitly sweeps in *"business cards, signs, insignias, letterheads, … internet advertising, web sites, social media …, billboards."* No medium escapes.

**Practical effect:** In Kansas, the team brand can be up to twice the size of "Van Noy Real Estate," and online the VNRE name must sit next to it. You cannot bury it in a footer the way you can in Missouri. **Design the KS-compliant version first; it auto-satisfies Missouri.** One template, both states.

## 4. NAR + Heartland MLS

You're a REALTOR in Heartland MLS, so two more layers apply on top of state law:

- **NAR Code of Ethics, Article 12 / SOP 12-5:** you may not advertise *"without disclosing the name of that REALTOR's firm in a reasonable and readily apparent manner."* **SOP 12-9:** websites must show the firm name and state(s) of licensure "in a reasonable and readily apparent manner." NAR's standard is "readily apparent," not "equal prominence" — so NAR is no stricter than Kansas here.

- **Heartland MLS Rules (Aug 2024):** when a **team name** goes in the First Listing Agent field, *"the … Participant or Subscriber within the Team having principal responsibility … shall be entered in the Second Listing Agent Field,"* and only same-brokerage licensees may appear there. Listing advertising by anyone other than the listing broker needs the listing broker's consent. **Clear Cooperation** counts team-branded sites and yard signs as "public marketing" — triggering MLS submission under VNRE within one business day. The MLS record always ties back to VNRE; the brokerage can't be erased from the listing data.

No NAR or Heartland rule blocks the model. They just reinforce: **brand the team, name the broker.**

## 5. How to build the VNRE version (compliant by design)

**Structure each branded team as a broker-owned DBA, not a separate brokerage.** This is the Side architecture and it's the cleanest path:

1. **VNRE holds the single license and all liability.** You are broker of record for every branded team. Their agents are licensed under VNRE.

2. **Each team's brand is a fictitious name registered to VNRE** with the Missouri SOS (RSMo §417.200; file a copy with MREC within 10 days per 20 CSR 2250-4.030). The brand is owned by VNRE — if a team leaves, negotiate brand portability up front (Side and Real both let teams keep the brand; decide your policy).

3. **The brand name avoids "realty / brokerage / company"** unless it incorporates "Van Noy Real Estate." Safer to coach teams toward names like *"The [X] Group"* / *"[X] Home Team"* that read as a team, not a brokerage. (Several states force "team"/"group" in the name; MO/KS don't mandate the word but the entity-word ban makes it the smart default.)

4. **One advertising template, Kansas-compliant:** team brand leads; "Brokered by Van Noy Real Estate" + VNRE phone, adjacent, no smaller than half the team-brand size, on every sign, site, card, and social profile. That clears KS (2× cap), MO (presence), NAR (readily apparent), and Heartland in one shot.

5. **Yard-sign tactic (Side's move):** route sign calls to the listing agent/team, not the brokerage — the law requires the broker *name*, and (in MO) the broker phone only when contact info appears. You control whose phone leads. (KS: VNRE name must be prominent/adjacent regardless.)

6. **Franchise/trade-name paperwork:** if any team operates under a licensed mark or external brand system, file that agreement with MREC (20 CSR 2250-8.080) and disclose franchisor non-liability in listing/closing docs if applicable.

**What you sell for the split:** tech stack (you already run FUB, Dotloop/Skyslope, BrokerBay, Mojo, Upfirst), compliance + transaction management (your VAs, the compliance-auditor workflow), DVN Coaching (your differentiator — Side has none), brand/logo build, lead-gen, and optional office space. That's the full "Side + Pinnacle + a real coach" package.

## 7. Risk & enforcement

- **You own the liability for every branded team.** NAR is explicit: the broker answers for how teams operate — branding, worker classification, and compensation are the three legal danger zones. White-labeling the *brand* does not white-label the *responsibility*.
- **Enforcement is real.** Other states have fined teams *and* their broker of record (failure to supervise) for misleading team names and missing broker info — e.g., a Pennsylvania case hit the team and brokerage for $5,000 total. MREC publishes discipline orders and treats improper advertising as a § 339.100 violation.
- **Supervision must scale.** More branded shingles = more advertising surface to police. Build an ad-approval checkpoint into onboarding; every team template gets reviewed against the KS-compliant standard before it goes live.

## 8. Decision filter

- **Drives revenue?** Yes — a split/fee on every branded producer is recurring GCI leverage on top of your solo listing business, and it monetizes the coaching you already do.
- **ROI in 90 days?** Slower — this is infrastructure. First branded team can launch fast; scale takes a recruiting motion.
- **Simple to run daily?** Only if templated. The compliance template + onboarding SOP is the difference between leverage and liability.
- **Adds or removes friction?** Adds supervision load; removes the recruiting ceiling of a single-brand brokerage.

**Recommendation:** Viable and differentiated. Build the **Kansas-compliant advertising template + the broker-owned-DBA onboarding SOP** first — those two artifacts *are* the product's legal spine. Pilot with one strong team before productizing. Confirm the exact MO/KS subsection text against the primary PDFs (below) before you publish any template, and have a one-time review with a MO/KS real estate attorney on the DBA-ownership and brand-portability terms — that's the one place the model breaks if the paperwork is wrong.
